What makes something living?

In Primary 3 Science, we learn to sort things into living and non-living. Living things share common characteristics: they need air, water and food, they grow, they can respond to changes, and they can reproduce (make more of their kind). Non-living things do not carry out all these life processes.

We use observable features to classify: for example, a cat grows and breathes (living), but a rock does not (non-living). Plants are living too — they make their own food and grow from seeds.
